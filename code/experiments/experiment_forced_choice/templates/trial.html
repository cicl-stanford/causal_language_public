Instructions

In this experiment, your task is to choose a description that best captures what happened in a video clip.

We will show you clips in which physical objects interact. All of the clips will take place in the rectangular space shown below. Objects will either start on screen or enter from the right. Objects can exit the space through the red exit on the left but only when it is not blocked by the brown gate. The position of the brown gate can be changed by pressing either of the green buttons next to it.

In each clip you will see some of the following four objects:

  • a green ball (labeled with A)
  • a grey ball (labeled with B)
  • an unlabeled blue ball
  • a box
Not all of the objects will appear in every clip, however every clip will contain ball A and ball B. In all of the clips, ball B will go through the red exit.

Below is an example clip. You can click on the "Play Video" button to watch.

In the experiment, we will ask you to view each clip at least twice. You may replay the clip as many times as you like.

Once you have watched the clip at least twice, we will ask you to choose which description best captures the clip you watched. You will be able to choose one of the following options:

  • Ball A caused Ball B to go through the red exit.
  • Ball A enabled Ball B to go through the red exit.
  • Ball A affected Ball B's going through the red exit.
  • Ball A made no difference to Ball B's going through the red exit.

When you are ready, go ahead and click the start button.
